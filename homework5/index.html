WOULD SEX RATIO AFFECT YOUR FUTURE LOVE LIFE?

 

Sex Ratio is the ratio of male to female in a population.

In a traditional household unit, there are two married individuals and their biological offspring.

To people living in a country with uneven sex ratio, how likely will their love life be affected?

 

 

SEX RATIO AROUND THE WORLD

 

 

What’s interesting?

 

In 2016, the average sex ratio (Male: Female) of the world is 1.01 (101:100). More than 153 countries with available data have a value below the average. It indicated the female and male population in the world are fairly the same.

Yet, Qatar, United Arab Emirates and Oman, three countries in the Middle East, have the most uneven sex ratio around the world. Around 75% of Qatar’s population is men, and both United Arab Emirates and Oman have a similar situation.

Although China and India are often the study subjects in related researches, they are off from the top 10 list. Hong Kong, where media outlets highlight its unequal sex ratio, turns out to be one of the countries with the most even sex ratio.

SEX RATIO AND CRUDE MARRIAGE RATE

 

 

Crude Marriage rate is the number of marriages occurring among the population of a given geographical area during a given year, per 1,000 mid-year total population of the given geographical area during the same year.

SEX RATIO AND FERTILITY RATE

 

 

Fertility rate is the average number of children that would be born to a woman over her lifetime.

 

Your possibility to get married or have a child?

 

Qatar, with the highest sex ratio in the world, has relatively low crude marriage and fertility rate. United Arab Emirates and Oath, which comes as the second and the third respectively, also lie among the lower tide.

However, most countries with an average sex ratio have a wide range of crude marriage and fertility rate. Take Mongolia and Germany as examples, although their sex ratio are both around 0.97, the former’s crude marriage rate is around the 4 times of the latter’s. Another example would be Malaysia and Niger, despite of the similar ratio around 1.06, Malaysia’s fertility rate is lower than Niger for about 5.

Since the places with extreme sex ratio are inadequate and there is a huge divergence between most countries, conclusion may not be drawn easily. Sex ratio may only be one of the factors affecting your future love life. Other social agents, such as custom and religion, should also be taken into account.
